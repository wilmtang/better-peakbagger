**Upgrade your Peakbagger.com experience.**

Better Peakbagger turns Garmin and Strava activities—or a GPX file you already have—into review-ready ascent drafts. It also adds 3D terrain, richer GPX analysis, faster ways to find useful ascent beta, a modern trip-report editor, optional GitHub backup, and a polished dark theme.

**Features**

- **Create ascent drafts from Garmin and Strava.** Open an activity you own and click the extension. Better Peakbagger finds likely summit encounters, explains each Strong or Probable match, and opens the ascents you choose as prefilled drafts.
- **Process a GPX on Peakbagger.** Choose a GPX file on an Add Ascent form to calculate distance, time, gain, multi-day details, and nearby summits. Large tracks are reduced to fit Peakbagger's limit, and multi-peak traverses can open coordinated drafts.
- **Review before saving.** GPS Preview can be prepared for you, but Better Peakbagger never clicks Save. You remain in control of every ascent published to your account.
- **Explore free 3D terrain.** View ascent routes, Full Screen GPS maps, and individual peaks in interactive 3D powered by [MapLibre](https://maplibre.org/) and open-data [Mapterhorn](https://mapterhorn.com/) elevation tiles. Use Peakbagger's compatible map layers, an optional OpenStreetMap-based vector layer, synchronized 2D/3D camera position, and an on-map compass. 3D is opt-in.
- **Understand GPX tracks.** Add interactive elevation charts, distance and time views, detailed route metrics, grades, local clock times, multi-day camping details, and chart-to-map tracking to ascent pages. Customize route color, width, casing, and map size.
- **Find useful ascent beta.** Filter ascent lists by trip report, GPS track, external link, or favorite climber, combine filters, and sort without reloading. Favorites can come from your Peakbagger Buddy List or a custom list you manage.
- **Write better trip reports.** Replace bracket-tag editing with a rich-text editor or GitHub-flavored Markdown, including formatting, tables, images, video, syntax highlighting, and live preview. Device-local autosave helps recover interrupted drafts, and Settings provides one place to review, copy, or remove them.
- **Back up your ascents to GitHub.** Optionally save one ascent or your full history to a repository you control, including the report as Markdown, entered fields as JSON, and Peakbagger's stored GPS track. You can also explicitly back up and restore a custom favorite-climber list.
- **Plan with better links.** Open summit-specific weather, recent satellite imagery, modeled snow depth where available, and fire/smoke information from supported peak pages.
- **Use Peakbagger your way.** Choose a site-wide light, dark, or system theme and adjust units, map behavior, GPX chart defaults, activity capture, the trip-report editor, and beta filters in one settings page.

**Privacy by design**

**No Better Peakbagger account. No analytics, telemetry, ads, or developer data server.**

[Read the complete privacy policy](https://github.com/wilmtang/better-peakbagger/blob/main/PRIVACY.md).

Better Peakbagger works with Chrome, Edge, Brave, and Firefox. It is an [independent, open-source project](https://github.com/wilmtang/better-peakbagger) and is not affiliated with Peakbagger.com, Garmin, Strava, GitHub, Mapterhorn, or the linked planning services.
